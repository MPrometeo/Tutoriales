% ============ LISTENING ============
\section*{Section 1: Listening (Preguntas 1--5)}
{\small\textit{Usar TTS (Google Translate / Natural Reader) para escuchar.
No leer mientras escuchas.}}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\textbf{\arabic*.}]

\item \textbf{W:} I thought the seminar started at 2.\\
\textbf{M:} It did, but they moved it to 3 starting this week.\\[3pt]
\textit{What does the man mean?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item The seminar was canceled
  \item The seminar time changed to 3
  \item The seminar is at 2 as usual
  \item He missed the seminar
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{M:} I've been looking for a parking spot for 20 minutes.\\
\textbf{W:} You should try the lot behind the science building.
Nobody parks there.\\[3pt]
\textit{What does the woman suggest?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item Driving to a different campus
  \item Using a less-known parking lot
  \item Taking the bus instead
  \item Arriving earlier tomorrow
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{W:} Professor Rios gave us a take-home exam instead
of an in-class final.\\
\textbf{M:} Lucky you. Ours is three hours in the auditorium.\\[3pt]
\textit{How does the man feel?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item He prefers in-class exams
  \item He wishes his exam were take-home too
  \item He thinks take-home exams are harder
  \item He already finished his final
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{M:} The tap water tastes strange today.\\
\textbf{W:} They're doing maintenance on the pipes. It should
be back to normal by tomorrow.\\[3pt]
\textit{What does the woman explain?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item The water is contaminated permanently
  \item Pipe maintenance is causing the taste temporarily
  \item They should buy bottled water
  \item The water has always tasted that way
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{W:} Did you know there are more stars in the
universe than grains of sand on Earth?\\
\textbf{M:} That's hard to wrap your head around, isn't it?\\[3pt]
\textit{What does the man mean by ``hard to wrap your head around''?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item It causes headaches
  \item It is difficult to comprehend
  \item It is not true
  \item He doesn't like astronomy
\end{enumerate}

\end{enumerate}

% ============ READING ============
\section*{Section 3: Reading (Preguntas 11--15)}

\textbf{Passage:}

\begin{quote}
\small
The domestication of maize (corn) is one of the most remarkable
achievements in agricultural history. Scientists believe that
maize was first domesticated from a wild grass called teosinte
in the Balsas River valley of southern Mexico approximately
9,000 years ago. Teosinte looks remarkably different from
modern corn---its kernels are tiny, hard, and encased in a
tough shell. Through thousands of years of selective breeding,
indigenous farmers transformed this modest grass into the large,
productive crop we know today. Maize became the foundation of
Mesoamerican civilizations, including the Olmec, Maya, and
Aztec, who considered it sacred. Today, maize is the world's
most widely produced grain, with over 1.1 billion tons grown
annually, used for food, animal feed, and biofuel.
\end{quote}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\textbf{\arabic*.}, start=11]

\item What is the passage mainly about?
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item Modern corn farming techniques
  \item The history of maize domestication from teosinte
  \item Why the Aztecs were powerful
  \item The Balsas River valley geography
\end{enumerate}

\item How does teosinte differ from modern corn?
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item It is larger and softer
  \item Its kernels are tiny, hard, and tough-shelled
  \item It grows only in water
  \item It was imported from Europe
\end{enumerate}

\item The word ``modest'' in the passage is closest in meaning to:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item impressive
  \item humble/unimpressive
  \item expensive
  \item colorful
\end{enumerate}

\item Which civilizations considered maize sacred?
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item Egyptian and Roman
  \item Olmec, Maya, and Aztec
  \item Inca and Mapuche
  \item Chinese and Indian
\end{enumerate}

\item It can be inferred that maize's transformation was:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]
  \item A quick, accidental process
  \item A gradual process over thousands of years of human selection
  \item Achieved only with modern genetic engineering
  \item Completed in the 20th century
\end{enumerate}

\end{enumerate}
